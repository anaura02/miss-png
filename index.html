Who
am I?

My name is May Hasola, a proud Bougainvillean and law student at the University of Papua New Guinea. Guided by my faith in Christ, I am passionate about empowering family values and inspiring positive change.

I believe true leadership begins with being a role model—uplifting others and shaping a future where confidence, integrity, and purpose drive Papua New Guinea forward.

I envision a Papua New Guinea where strong families build strong communities, where women and children are empowered to dream fearlessly, and where our values shape a brighter, united future.

Change doesn’t happen overnight, but with faith, determination, and a little courage, we can create a nation where every voice is heard, every potential is realized, and every family thrives. The future isn’t just something we wait for—it’s something we build together.

Let’s start now!

Why am I here?

I come from a loving family where faith, hard work, and service shaped who I am. As a strong Christian, I was taught that success isn’t just about personal achievements but about lifting others up.

From a young age, I pushed myself to be the best, excelling in school and dreaming of a future where I could serve Papua New Guinea. I’ve seen the struggles our people face, and I believe in using my voice, education, and faith to create change.

I am here to inspire, to empower, and to help build a stronger, united PNG—where dreams are possible for everyone.
